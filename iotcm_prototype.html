What each sensor measures — and why it matters
Soil moisture
Measures volumetric water content (%) in the soil. Critical for detecting saturation events that reduce effective stress and can trigger slope failure or crib wall instability. Also governs plant-available water for establishing vegetation.
Typical range: 10–70% VWC  ·  Alert: >55–65% (structure-dependent)
Soil pH
Monitors soil acidity and alkalinity on a 0–14 scale. Most bioengineering plant species (willow, alder, pioneer grasses) require pH 5.5–7.5. Outside this range, nutrient uptake is impaired and root development fails, compromising long-term structural reinforcement.
Optimal: 5.5–7.5  ·  Alert: <5.0 or >8.0
Displacement
Detects millimetre-scale movement at structural joints, wall toe, or slope surface using tilt sensors or MEMS accelerometers. Provides early warning of settlement, creep, or incipient failure days or weeks before it becomes visible — essential for preventive maintenance.
Typical range: 0–20 mm  ·  Alert: >5 mm cumulative
Soil temperature
Tracks soil temperature at depth. Needed to correctly interpret moisture sensor outputs (temperature affects dielectric readings). Also monitors frost penetration depth, which can damage root systems, cause frost heave in structural elements, and trigger freeze–thaw erosion.
Range: −5 to +40 °C  ·  Monitor for freeze/thaw cycles

Documentation
Scope, methodology, assumptions, and limitations of the IoTCM prototype.
Purpose

The IoT Conceptual Map (IoTCM) tool provides a standardised conceptual framework for sensor-based monitoring of bioengineering structures such as live crib walls, vegetated slopes, riverbanks, and fascine drains. It addresses the absence of long-term, data-driven performance records by defining critical measurement parameters and a cost-effective IoT architecture accessible to engineers, environmental authorities, researchers, and NGOs.

Scope
  • Sensor placement guidance per structure type
  • Parameter threshold definition
  • IoT hardware recommendations by budget
  • Aggregated health index scoring
  • EU NRL compliance indicators
Monitored parameters
  • Soil moisture (% volumetric water content)
  • Soil pH
  • Structural displacement (mm)
  • Soil temperature (°C)
  • Optional: pore water pressure, rainfall
Assumptions

Sensor readings are assumed to be calibrated within manufacturer specifications. Health index thresholds are based on generalised literature values and should be adapted to site-specific conditions before deployment. This prototype uses simulated data; a production version would connect to a live IoT backend such as ThingsBoard or AWS IoT Core.

Limitations

This tool does not replace site-specific geotechnical assessment or expert consultation. It does not account for extreme weather events, sensor fouling, or sensor failure. Spatial analysis is schematic rather than GIS-based. The EU NRL compliance check is indicative only and does not substitute official reporting obligations. All data shown in this prototype is simulated.
